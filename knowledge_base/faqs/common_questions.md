## SAFARI DETAILS

**Q13: What time does the safari start?**
A: It depends on the package:
- **Nairobi National Park:** Pickups begin at **6:30 AM** from Nairobi CBD or the park main gate on Langata Road
- **Masai Mara 3-day:** Depart Nairobi around **6:00 AM** on Day 1

We'll confirm your exact pickup time based on your hotel location when you book.

---

**Q14: Where is the pickup point?**
A: We pick up from your hotel in Nairobi. We cover: CBD, Westlands, Kilimani, Upper Hill,
and surrounding areas. Provide your hotel name and address at booking and we'll confirm
your pickup time.

Airport pickup is a separate arrangement — please enquire at booking.

---

**Q15: What should I wear / pack?**
A: Key items:
- **Clothing:** Neutral tones (khaki, olive, beige, brown) — avoid white and bright colours.  
  Nairobi NP: casual clothes are fine. Masai Mara: neutral clothes matter more in open vehicles.
- Layers — mornings can be cold (especially Mara at 10–15°C), afternoons hot
- Sunscreen, sunglasses, hat
- Insect repellent for Masai Mara (malaria zone)
- Camera and binoculars if you have them

Full packing lists are included with your booking confirmation.

---

**Q16: What's included in the price?**
A: Included: Transport, guide, and window seat.
For **Masai Mara**: accommodation and all meals (Full Board) are also included.
For **Nairobi NP**: no meals (half day).
Park fees are typically quoted separately and may be included if you select them at booking.
See individual package pages for full inclusion/exclusion lists.

---

**Q17: Is food included?**
A: **Nairobi National Park:** No — it's a half-day safari, no meals included.
**Masai Mara 3-day:** Yes! All accommodation is **Full Board** — breakfast, lunch,
and dinner are all included. Drinks (alcohol, sodas) are extra.

---

**Q18: Are park fees included in the price?**
A: Park fees can be bundled into the total or quoted separately. When you receive a
quote, we'll show transport, accommodation, and park fees as separate line items.
You'll pay all costs before departure — nothing extra to pay at the gate.

---

**Q19: Do I need travel insurance?**
A: Yes — we strongly recommend it and some bookings may require it. Your policy
must include medical evacuation coverage. Evacuation from remote safari areas
can cost USD 15,000–50,000+. Please purchase insurance before travelling.

We don't sell insurance but can recommend categories to look for. Popular providers
for safari travellers: World Nomads, Allianz, AXA.

---

**Q20: What type of vehicle do you use?**
A: We use 4x4 safari vehicles — typically Toyota Land Cruisers or similar. These are
specially adapted with pop-top roofs for standing up during game drives and window
seats for every passenger. Maximum 6–7 passengers per vehicle.

---

**Q21: How many people will be in the group?**
A: **Nairobi NP:** Maximum 6 people per vehicle.
**Masai Mara:** Maximum 6–7 people per vehicle.

Because we operate group joining, you may share with other travellers you haven't
met before. Minimum group for guaranteed departure: 1 person (we guarantee
departures regardless of numbers).

---

**Q22: Can I request a window seat?**
A: Yes — and it's automatic. We guarantee a window seat for every passenger. No one
sits in the middle. This is one of our core commitments.

---

**Q23: What wildlife will we see?**
A: We can tell you what is commonly and possibly seen, but we cannot guarantee any
specific animal sighting — wildlife is wild and unpredictable. See the individual
package guides for detailed wildlife expectations based on season and destination.

What we CAN guarantee: a trained, experienced guide who knows the park well and
will work hard to find you the best sightings.

---

**Q24: Is it safe?**
A: Yes. Park safety concerns in Kenya safari parks are very low. Standard rules apply:
- Stay in the vehicle unless a guide confirms it is safe to disembark
- Do not hand-feed or approach wildlife
- Follow your guide's instructions at all times

Kenya's national parks and reserves are well-managed and have professional ranger
presence. Our guides are trained and experienced.

## HEALTH & PREPARATION

**Q31: Do I need malaria tablets?**
A: The Masai Mara is in a malaria zone. We strongly recommend consulting a travel
health clinic or GP about malaria prophylaxis before travel. Common options include
Malarone, Doxycycline, and Lariam — your doctor will advise what's right for you.

Nairobi is at altitude (~1,800m) and generally considered low malaria risk in the
city, but the parks are different.

We don't make specific medication recommendations — always consult a medical professional.

---

**Q32: Do I need vaccinations?**
A: For Kenya, commonly recommended vaccinations include: Hepatitis A, Typhoid, Yellow
Fever (if arriving from a Yellow Fever endemic country), and keeping routine
vaccinations (tetanus, MMR) up to date. Some nationalities must present a valid Yellow
Fever certificate to enter Kenya.

Always check the current requirements for your nationality via your government's
travel advisory website, and consult a travel health clinic.

---

**Q33: What is the weather like?**
A: Kenya has two main rainy seasons:
- **Long rains:** March–May (heavier, can affect roads)
- **Short rains:** October–November (lighter)

Best weather: **January–February** and **June–September** (dry, warm, excellent game viewing).

The Masai Mara at altitude can be cold at night year-round (10–15°C evenings/mornings).
Afternoons are hot and sunny during dry season.

---

**Q34: Is there WiFi at the camps?**
A: WiFi is limited or unavailable at most bush camps in the Masai Mara. Power for
charging devices is available during limited hours (usually via solar or generator).
Bring a power bank. This is an opportunity to truly disconnect.

---

**Q35: What currency should I bring to Kenya?**
A: US Dollars are widely accepted in the tourism sector. Kenya Shillings are used
locally. ATMs in Nairobi dispense KES. For tips and small purchases in the park,
both USD and KES work.

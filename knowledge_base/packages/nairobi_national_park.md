# Nairobi National Park — Safari Package Guide

## Package: Half Day Nairobi National Park Group Safari

### Overview
Nairobi National Park is one of the world's most unique wildlife experiences: big game
roaming free against the backdrop of a modern city skyline. Located just 7km from
Nairobi's CBD, the park is Kenya's oldest national park and a critical rhino sanctuary.

The half-day safari is our most popular entry-level package — perfect for travellers
with limited time, those transiting through Nairobi, or anyone wanting an affordable
taste of the African bush before heading to longer safaris.

**Key selling points:**
- Only national park in the world adjacent to a capital city
- Home to approximately 70 black rhinos — one of the best places in East Africa to see them
- Over 400 bird species
- No overnight stay needed — works around any travel itinerary

## Wildlife Guide

### Commonly Seen
Lions (Nairobi NP has a healthy pride population), Maasai giraffes, Common (Burchell's)
zebras, Blue wildebeest, Cape buffaloes, Warthogs, Impalas, Thomson's gazelles,
Grant's gazelles, Common elands, Olive baboons, Ostriches.

**Birds:** Marabou stork, secretary bird, Egyptian goose, various eagles, vultures,
kingfishers, sunbirds — 400+ species total.

### Possibly Seen (Rarer)
**Black rhinos** — the park is a rhino sanctuary with ~70 individuals. Sightings are
possible but not guaranteed. If seeing rhinos is a priority, let us know — guides have
best knowledge of current locations.

Leopards, Cheetahs, Spotted hyenas, Hippos (in the dam area).

### What We Don't Guarantee
No safari guide can promise specific animal sightings — wildlife is wild and unpredictable.
What we DO guarantee: a professional guide who knows the park thoroughly and will
maximise your chances of great sightings.

## About the Giraffe Centre (Add-On)

The African Fund for Endangered Wildlife's Giraffe Centre is a 5-minute drive from
the NNP main gate. Home to the endangered Rothschild's giraffe, visitors can hand-feed
these magnificent animals from an elevated platform. Educational and highly photogenic.

**Entry:** USD 15/person (non-resident)
**Duration:** ~45-60 minutes
**Best combined with:** Morning departure

## About David Sheldrick Wildlife Trust (Add-On)

The world's most successful elephant orphan rescue and rehabilitation programme.
Baby elephants (some just weeks old) are cared for here before being reintegrated into
the wild in Tsavo. Feeding time at 11am is deeply moving.

**Entry:** USD 20/person
**Critical:** Open to visitors 11:00–12:00 ONLY — must be timed with morning departure
**Duration:** 1 hour exactly
**Advance registration:** Recommended via sheldrickwildlifetrust.org (though walk-in
often possible)

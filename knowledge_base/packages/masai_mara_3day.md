## Package: 3 Days / 2 Nights Masai Mara Game Reserve Group Safari

### Overview
The Masai Mara is Kenya's most iconic safari destination — and for good reason. This
vast grassland ecosystem on the northern edge of the Serengeti-Mara ecosystem hosts
one of the largest Big Five populations in Africa and is the stage for the world's
greatest wildlife spectacle: the Great Migration.

Our 3-day group joining safari is the most popular multiday package. Travellers share
a vehicle (max 7 people), share costs, and share an experience that very few people
in the world have had.

## Wildlife Guide

### Commonly Seen in the Mara
**Big Five:** Lion ✓ Elephant ✓ Cape Buffalo ✓ Leopard ✓ (higher density than most
parks) Rhino (black rhino — rare but present)

**Other highlights:** Cheetahs, Spotted hyenas, Black-backed jackals, Bat-eared foxes,
Maasai giraffes, Common zebras, Blue wildebeest, Topi, Hartebeest, Common elands,
Defassa waterbucks, Hippos (Mara River), Nile crocodiles (Mara River), Warthogs, Olive
baboons, Vervet monkeys, 450+ bird species

### The Great Migration
The annual migration of over 1.5 million wildebeest and hundreds of thousands of
zebras between Tanzania's Serengeti and Kenya's Masai Mara is the largest overland
migration of mammals on Earth.

**When is the migration in the Mara?**
- **July–October** — The herds are in the Mara, crossing the Mara River repeatedly
- **August–September** — Peak river crossings (most dramatic, most unpredictable)
- **November–June** — Herds are south in Tanzania's Serengeti

**What to tell customers about river crossings:**
The crossings are completely wild and unpredictable — they happen when the animals
decide, not on schedule. Your guide will monitor radio communication between park
guides to find where crossings are happening. Witnessing a crossing is extraordinarily
special but cannot be guaranteed even during peak months.

## Frequently Asked Questions — Masai Mara Specific

**Q: Is the Great Migration worth it in July vs. August vs. September?**
A: All three months are excellent. August has the most consistent and dramatic crossings.
July is the start — exciting, often uncrowded. September is peak but also peak crowds.

**Q: What if I travel outside July–October? Is it still worth going?**
A: Absolutely. The Mara has resident Big Five year-round. You'll just miss the river
crossings, but lion density, leopard sightings, cheetah, and general game viewing are
excellent all year. January–March and June are particularly good.

**Q: Is the drive from Nairobi rough?**
A: The tarmac road (A104) to Narok is smooth. The final section into the park
is unpaved and can be bumpy, especially during rains. 4x4 vehicles handle it well.
Drive total: approximately 5–6 hours including breaks.

**Q: What are the rains like?**
A: Long rains: April–May. Short rains: October–November. During rains, some roads
can become muddy and challenging. However, wildlife viewing is often excellent (animals
are active, vegetation is lush and photogenic). Rains are typically short afternoon
showers, not all-day downpours.

**Q: Do I need a visa for Kenya?**
A: Most nationalities need a Kenya e-Visa (apply at evisa.go.ke, currently ~USD 50 for
single entry). Some nationalities still receive visa on arrival. Always confirm the
current requirement for your nationality well in advance of travel. Your passport must
be valid for at least 6 months beyond your travel dates.

**Q: Is it safe?**
A: The Masai Mara is one of Kenya's most visited and well-managed national parks.
Our professional guides are trained and experienced. Standard safari safety rules apply:
do not open vehicle windows for selfies with wildlife, follow guide instructions at all
times, stay in designated areas. The drive from Nairobi is on normal roads.
